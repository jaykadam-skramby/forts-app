Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort was the capital of the Maratha Empire from 1674 to 1818. It is located on a hilltop in the Sahyadri Mountains and is considered to be one of the most important forts in Indian history. The fort is well-preserved and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Raigad Fort, also known as Rairi or Rairy Fort, is a magnificent hill fort situated in the Sahyadri mountain range, overlooking the town of Mahad in the Raigad district of Maharashtra. Standing tall at an elevation of 2,700 feet (820 meters) above its base and 4,449 feet (1,356 meters) above sea level, Raigad Fort offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Historical Significance

Raigad Fort holds immense historical significance as it served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under the legendary Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. In 1674, Shivaji was crowned the king of the Maratha kingdom, and Raigad Fort became the center of his administrative and military operations. During his reign, the Maratha Empire flourished and expanded considerably.

Architectural Features

Raigad Fort is a testament to the architectural brilliance of the Maratha era. Its construction was overseen by Shivaji himself, alongside his chief engineer, Hiroji Indulkar. The fort's layout is designed for defense, with multiple layers of fortification, bastions, and gateways.

Accessing Raigad Fort

Visitors can climb approximately 1,737 steps, enjoying a scenic and invigorating experience. Alternatively, the Raigad Ropeway provides quick access to the top via an aerial tramway spanning 2,460 feet (750 meters).

Visiting Hours

Raigad Fort is open from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. The best time to visit is between October and March for pleasant weather and stunning views.
