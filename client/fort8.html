Purandar Fort

Purandar Fort, located in Pune district, is a majestic hill fort that played a key role during the rise of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It is historically known as the birthplace of Sambhaji Maharaj and the site of the Treaty of Purandar (1665).

The fort consists of two parts‚ÄîPurandar (the upper fort) and Vajragad (the lower fort). Surrounded by greenery and steep valleys, it offers a stunning view of the Sahyadri range.

Historical Significance

In 1665, Shivaji Maharaj signed the Treaty of Purandar here with the Mughals. It was an important turning point in Swarajya‚Äôs early years. It also served as a military training ground during British rule.

Architectural Features

The fort has robust bastions, temples (like Kedareshwar and Narayaneshwar), and water tanks. Stone-carved stairways connect the fortifications, and cannons are placed at key viewpoints.

Accessing Purandar Fort

Purandar is around 40 km from Pune. You can drive to the base village (Narayanpur) and trek around 1.5‚Äì2 hours to the top. Entry may be restricted on some days due to Army control.

Visiting Hours

Open from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM (only on permitted days). Check with local officials or forest department in advance before visiting.
