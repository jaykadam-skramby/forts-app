Murud-Janjira Fort

Murud-Janjira Fort is located on an island off the coastal village of Murud in Raigad district. Known for its strong fortifications, it is famous for being the only fort on the west coast of India that remained unconquered by enemies, including the Marathas and the British.

Originally built by Abyssinian Siddis in the 15th century, it later became a symbol of naval dominance and maritime defense.

Historical Significance

Despite repeated attacks by Shivaji Maharaj and later Sambhaji Maharaj, Murud-Janjira never fell. Its defenders used naval might, tactical diplomacy, and a fortress that withstood even cannon bombardments.

Architectural Features

Janjira features 19 rounded bastions, secret underwater entrances, sweet water tanks, and the mighty Kalal Bangadi cannon. The architecture is a blend of Indo-Arabic influence.

Accessing Murud-Janjira

The fort is accessible only by boat from Rajapuri Jetty. It's about 2 km from the Murud village and 165 km from Mumbai via road.

Visiting Hours

Open daily from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Boats operate from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM depending on tides and weather.
