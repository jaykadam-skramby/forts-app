Sindhudurg Fort

Sindhudurg Fort is situated on an island off the Malvan coast in Sindhudurg district. Constructed by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1664, it stands as a monumental maritime defense fort.

Built using laterite rocks and sand mixed with lead, it was designed to withstand strong tides and enemy assaults, marking a brilliant example of Maratha naval foresight.

Historical Significance

Sindhudurg was crucial in protecting the Konkan coastline from foreign invasions. It served as the naval headquarters for the Marathas and holds Shivaji Maharaj's handprint and footprint preserved in stone.

Architectural Features

The fort features 3 km-long ramparts, zigzag entrances for defense, and hidden water tanks. The fort is accessible only by boat, emphasizing its secure and strategic placement.
