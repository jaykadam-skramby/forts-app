Rajgad Fort

Rajgad Fort, meaning "King of Forts," was the first capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Located in the Pune district, it stands at an altitude of 1,400 meters and offers stunning views of the Sahyadri mountain ranges.

The fort witnessed many significant events including Shivaji's coronation preparations, birth of his son Rajaram, and the burial of his queen Saibai. It was the administrative capital for more than 25 years before the capital shifted to Raigad.

Historical Significance

Built on the Murumbadevi Dongar, Rajgad was strategically chosen by Shivaji due to its formidable structure, natural defenses, and isolated location. The fort remained a central point for many battles and planning operations.

Architectural Features

Rajgad Fort has three major sub-plateaus: Padmavati Machi, Suvela Machi, and Sanjeevani Machi. Major structures include the Padmavati Temple, Balekilla (citadel), Darwajas (gates), water cisterns, and palaces. It is a trekker‚Äôs paradise.
