Vijaydurg Fort

Vijaydurg Fort, located in Sindhudurg district near Devgad, is one of the oldest and strongest sea forts built by the Marathas. Originally constructed by the Shilahar dynasty, it was later significantly fortified by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Known as the "Eastern Gibraltar," it served as a key naval base and witnessed the rise of the Maratha Navy under Kanhoji Angre.

Historical Significance

Vijaydurg played a pivotal role in protecting the western coast. Shivaji Maharaj added triple-layered walls, underwater defenses, and watchtowers to make it impregnable to enemy ships.

Architectural Features

With massive laterite walls, 27 bastions, a secret dockyard, and strategic elevation, Vijaydurg stands as a maritime engineering marvel. It also features a 200-meter-long undersea wall visible during low tide.

Accessing Vijaydurg Fort

The fort is accessible by road from Devgad (30 km). Nearest railway station: Kankavli or Rajapur. No boat ride needed ‚Äì the fort is connected to the mainland.
