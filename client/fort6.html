Pratapgad Fort

Pratapgad Fort, located in Satara district near Mahabaleshwar, is famous for the legendary battle between Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan in 1659. It stands as a symbol of Maratha bravery and tactical genius.

Built strategically on the hilltop, the fort offers commanding views and is divided into upper and lower sections, showcasing brilliant Maratha fortification.

Historical Significance

Architectural Features

The fort features a grand Mahadarwaja, bastions, secret escape routes, and a bronze statue of Shivaji Maharaj. Temples and water tanks are strategically located across the fort.

Accessing Pratapgad Fort

It is accessible by road from Mahabaleshwar (approx. 25 km). Vehicles reach close to the fort entrance, and light trekking or walking completes the visit.

Visiting Hours

Open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Weekends are popular for historical tours and sightseeing.
