Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad Fort, meaning ‚ÄúLion‚Äôs Fort,‚Äù is one of the most famous forts in Maharashtra. Located around 35 km from Pune, it sits atop a cliff in the Sahyadri ranges, offering panoramic views and a deep sense of Maratha history.

The fort is best known for the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where Tanaji Malusare, Shivaji Maharaj's trusted commander, laid down his life recapturing the fort from the Mughals. It is a place that echoes with the cries of bravery and devotion.

Historical Significance

Originally known as Kondhana, the fort changed hands multiple times before it was recaptured by Shivaji Maharaj. Tanaji's sacrifice became legendary, prompting Shivaji to say, "Gad ala pan Sinha gela" (The fort is won, but the lion is lost).

Architectural Features

The fort features solid stone walls, bastions, temples, gates, and memorials. Tanaji‚Äôs memorial and tomb lie atop the fort. The Devtake (natural spring), Amruteshwar temple, and military storage buildings can still be explored today.

Accessing Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad is accessible by road and trek. Trekking from the base village takes around 1.5 to 2 hours. Vehicles can go halfway up the hill as well. It is a popular spot for morning hikes and nature lovers.

Visiting Hours

Sinhagad Fort is open from 5:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Early morning visits offer the best views and a pleasant trekking experience.
