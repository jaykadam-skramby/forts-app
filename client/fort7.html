Lohagad Fort

Lohagad Fort, located near Lonavala in the Pune district, is one of the most accessible and scenic forts in Maharashtra. Known as the 'Iron Fort', it stands strong at 1033 meters above sea level.

The fort was actively used by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj during the Maratha reign, and it played a strategic role in guarding the nearby Visapur Fort and trade routes.

Historical Significance

Lohagad was captured by Shivaji Maharaj in 1648, and again in 1670 after a brief Mughal occupation. It served as a key treasury and military base for the Marathas.

Accessing Lohagad Fort

It is easily accessible from Lonavala and Malavli stations. The trek is beginner-friendly and especially popular during the monsoon for its lush green surroundings.

Visiting Hours

Open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Monsoon and winter seasons are best for visiting due to cool climate and picturesque views.
